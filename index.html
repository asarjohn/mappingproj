Citi Bike Took A While to Come to Black Brooklyn. Now It's Here... Somewhat.

By Asar John

December 15, 2022

When Citi Bike was launched in 2013, its first set of bikes in Brooklyn were placed in wealthier and whiter areas of the borough.

In 2019, Lyft, the company that acquired Citi Bike in 2018, announced that an expansion of the bike system would be introduced as a five-year plan. This expansion would place bike stations in several black and brown communities that did not have direct access to Citi Bike. A short time before this announcement, a report from New York Communities for Change conducted a study that put Citi Bike on the spotlight for not serving these communities for years since the original rollout.

When asked if race or wealth have anything to do with what areas are expanded to, a spokesperson for the NYC Department of Transportation said DOT believes the growth of the system is based on equity and the 2013 launch to parts of Brooklyn and Manhattan were related to population density and an abundance of safe bicycle infrastructure.
